A spokesman for the Navy of the Armed Forces of Ukraine, the 3rd rank, Dmytro Pletenchuk reported on the morning of March 26, 2024 that he was captured by the Ukrainian large landing ship "Konstantin Olshansky" in 2014 by the Russian occupiers were struck by the Ukrainian Neptune rocket.

Dmitry Pletenchuk made a corresponding statement duringlive etherOn the Ukrainian TV channel FREEDD.

According to him, a detective story was unfolding around the great landing ship "Konstantin Olshansky".

"I remind you, it is a large Ukrainian landing ship, which at 14 years ago was captured by Russian invaders," said the Switch of the Navy of the Armed Forces.

For 9 years, Konstantin Olshansky, according to Dmitry Pletenchuk, stood in the Sevastopol Bay.

“And it was disassembled for spare parts. Just robbed. And in the 10th year of the war, they realized that they ended with large landing ships of this project 775, ”the spokesman said.

According to him, the occupiers decided to restore the Konstantin Olshansky ship.

“And during the year he was piled up, carrying out restorative work. But the purpose of these works, as we understand, was to make falsification. In order to report to the Moscow leadership and thus consumers of information that they do not understand anything, in Russia and in the occupied territories about how they resumed one of their great landing ones, which were previously damaged. It could be either "Minsk" or "Olenogorsky Mine", "Dmitry Pletenchuk said.

In the "Minsk" and "Olenogorsky Mine", according to the spokesman of the Navy of the Armed Forces, the nature of the damage is such that their restoration under a big question.

“And, accordingly, there was such an idea - to give our ship for one of them. Accordingly, this ship was to be used against Ukraine. Unfortunately. Therefore, a decision was made to defeat this unit by our "Neptune". The lesion was caused. Damage is clarified. He also suffered damage. In any case, he is also not ammunition either. These are the news for the morning, ”Dmitry Pletenchuk summarized.
