Iceland has made the decision to join the Czech initiative to purchase ammunition for Ukraine. The country will allocate almost 2 million euros.

About thisinformsIceland Government Press Service.

"There was not enough ammunition, and in cooperation with the main partners of Iceland, the Czech Republic has committed to ensure ammunition artillery of Ukraine, which plays a key role in the country's defense," the statement said.

Foreign Minister Bjarny Benedictsson said such assistance is a support for international law and the protection of his or her own interests in the field of security.

"It is extremely important that we continue to strongly support Ukraine, but so we contribute not only to the protection of the people of the country, but also to international law in a broader context and our own security interests," he said.

Iceland will also support the purchase of equipment for women in the Ukrainian army. The percentage of women in the army has increased significantly in recent years, especially after the Russian invasion. To meet the needs of women servicemen, it is proposed to provide uniforms, bulletproof vests, medical and hygienic products for 75 million crowns (half a million euros).

The department noted that this decision would have to be approved by Parliament.

As reportedArmyinform, Luxembourg joins the Czech initiative to purchase ammunition in support of the Armed Forces of Ukraine.
