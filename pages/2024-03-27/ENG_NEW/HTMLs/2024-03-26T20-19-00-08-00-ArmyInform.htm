Czech Foreign Minister Jan Lipavsky called a figure of 1.5 million shells as a potential volume that could be set on the AMCU Initiative to provide the Armed Forces of the Armed Forces.

About this Jan LipavskayaHe said in an interviewBloomberg.

The Czech initiative provided for the purchase of approximately 800,000 shells outside the EU.

"We can do a lot more than an initially declared number," said Jan Lipavsky. In his opinion, it may be about supplying 1.5 million shells.

Although the first "Czech" shells have not yet come to Ukraine, the initiative itself is already working.

"As we can see, it already helps Ukraine to fight better because they know that they will have stocks of fresh ammunition, which has changed their view of the use of current stocks," Lipavsky said.

Asreported the armyinform, in the Czech Republic, Ukraine will receive the first shells within the Czech initiative in June.
