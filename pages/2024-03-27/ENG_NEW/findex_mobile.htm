What is this missile?

During development, it was projected as an anti -ship (PCR) of a ship base. This is a conditionally maneuverable hypersonic cruise missile.

Control system: INS + ARLGSN (active radar homing head). Possible types of warhead (BC) - high-explosive, penetrating, nuclear, warhead weight - 300–500 kg. The main purpose is the overwhelming ships of the enemy of different classes - from frigates to aircraft carriers, as well as its terrestrial military sites.

The rocket is launched on the so -called mortar start.

Reference.A mortar launch (or "cold" start) is a way to start a rocket during which a rocket is ejected from the starting installation or TPK (transport-public container) due to the pressure created in a closed volume by any source located outside the missile .

Such a source can be, for example, a powder pressure accumulator (PJSC) or a steam and gas generator. In this case, the rocket engine starts after the rocket leaves the launcher.

After the rocket leaves the TPK, the acceleration stage with solid-fuel engines is turned on, which gives primary supersonic acceleration, after which the ramjet engine on liquid fuel (decillin) accelerates it to a speed of 4 M (M—Mach number, Mach is conventionally equal to 340 m/s or 1224 km/h).

After the "mortar" launch and the exit of the rocket from the silo, the acceleration stage begins to work, which ensures the launch of the Zircon to a considerable height and its primary acceleration to speeds at which a hypersonic ramjet engine can begin to operate.

Already at its expense, Zircon, flying at an altitude of several tens of kilometers, reaches a constant speed of up to 5.5 m in the marching section of the flight.

In the target area, the Zircon begins a dive and briefly accelerates to 7.5 M. At this time, the missile body heats up to the maximum temperatures that a special thermal insulation coating allows it to withstand. At the same time, closer to the ground, the speed of Zircon is no longer hypersonic, although it remains ultra-high — 4.5 M.

The Ukrainian hospitals, primary health care centers, outpatients, perinatal centers and oncological dispensaries will receive 73 generators with a capacity of 10.8-240 kW for uninterrupted operation under the conditions of possible power outages.

About thisreported March 26Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine.

The equipment is purchased within the framework of a joint project of the Ministry of Health of Ukraine and the World Bank "Emergency Response to the Covid-19 and Vaccination in Ukraine".

The generators are divided into Ternopil, Rivne, Chernihiv, Kharkiv, Khmelnytsky, Volyn, Ivano-Frankivsk, Vinnytsia, Poltava, Kirovograd, Dnipropetrovsk, Odessa, Sumy, Zhytomyr, Lviv regions and the city of Kyiv.

Given the constant attacks and firing of Russia's energy infrastructure of Ukraine, providing health care institutions with generators will allow hospitals to work smoothly under any critical conditions.
