The Polish Army is modernizing its tactical reconnaissance capabilities by signing a contract with the domestic vehicle developer AMZ-Kutno for the supply of Kleszcz wheeled patrol vehicles, which are based on the Bóbr 3 light armored reconnaissance transports (LOTR).

The newest kleszcz (Tick - Ukrainian) intended to replace the old SovietBrdm-2In particular, modernized Poland (RP) BRDM-2 M96/M97, reports the Armyinform.

The LOTR project is an element of the Polish Operational Patrol Intelligence program, which is part of a broader plan for the technical modernization of the Armed Forces of the Republic of Poland. The contract, signed at the end of winter 2024, provides for the purchase of 286 light armored reconnaissance aircraft, which will be delivered from 2025 to 2035.

Tick ​​(the earlier name-Bór-3)) will be armed with the intelligence units of the Land Forces of the RP. The machine should provide operation for five days in any weather and at any time of day and night.

Kleszcz uses a proprietary 4×4 wheel arrangement powered by a 240 kW diesel powertrain mated to an Allison automatic transmission. It has a speed of 90 km/h and the ability to move off-road. The armor provides protection against ballistic threats for the 4569 STANAG Level 2 (7.62×39mm bullet at a distance of 30 meters), as well as against Level 2A (6 kg) mine explosions.

In addition to reconnaissance missions, LOTR allows you to provide fire support thanks to a remotely controlled combat module equipped with 12.7 and 7.62 mm machine guns and capable of combating lightly armored vehicles, low-flying air targets and enemy manpower.

The machine is completely amphibious at the speed of movement on water up to 7 km/h, which facilitates intelligence, despite rivers and water obstacles.

The car will carry five soldiers, including the driver. This will allow the intelligence group when necessary. The car was shown at defense exhibitions, equipped with a telescopic mast, which installed intelligence and observation sensors.

This is probably the electro -optical "Day/Night" electrical sensors, a laser rangefinder and a tactical land radar. In addition, Kleszcz is reported to carry, deploy and control one or both unmanned aerial and land devices for remote surveillance.

ForConclusionsAccording to Steven W. Miller, an analyst at Armada International, the new Polish armored vehicle is likely to have onboard navigation and positioning, as well as secure communications and the ability to analyze and transmit intelligence.

"This acquisition demonstrates the recognition of the importance of advanced battlefield intelligence, as demonstrated during the war in Ukraine," Stephen W. Miller concluded.

The development and production of an armored vehicle took place in the cooperation of the AMZ-Kutno S.A, the Military Institute of Chemistry and Radiometry, the Military Institute of Communication and the Military Institute of Armament Technology of the Republic of Poland.

The total cost of the Kleszcz armored vehicle development project is zł36.45 million zlotys (close to ₴357.5 million).
