The EU Council spoke about the creation of a military assistance fund for Ukraine in the amount of 5 billion euros for 2024 as part of the reformed European Peace Fund, and also named the priority areas for the use of these funds.

About thisIt is saidon the site of the European Council.

"The newly created Ukrainian assistance fund will allow the EU to continue to maintain the growing needs of the Armed Forces of Ukraine by providing both lethal and non -lethal military equipment, as well as training," the statement reads.

The creation of the Ukrainian Assistance Fund aims to bring to the maximum added cost of using these resources, in the measurement of delivery of greater and better prompt support for Ukraine, in addition to bilateral efforts in this area at the level of Member States. At the same time, attention will be focused on increasing the fate of the joint order from the enterprises of the European and Norwegian defense industry.

In parallel, the EU Council has decided to further improve the rules of management of the European Peace Fund, increase their reliability and adapt to new international circumstances.

Such new management rules will be based on the effective structures of the defense order at the national level, as well as on existing framework contracts, which were concluded with the help of so-called leadership countries, the European Defense Agency and Norway. Such joint orders will include small and medium -sized enterprises belonging to the European and Norwegian defense industry.

The EU emphasizes that this approach will contribute to more flexibility in supply chains that may include operators or enterprises outside the EU or Norway.

"After today's decision, the financial ceiling for the European Peace Fund is set at the level of more than 17 billion euros for the period 2021-2027," the statement reads.

The high representative of the EU Josep Borrel noted that with the help of this EU fund will continue to support Ukraine in its efforts from self -defense against the Russian aggressive war, which would not need it, and as long as it will be necessary.
