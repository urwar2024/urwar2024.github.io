The court found it guilty and sentenced to 15 years in prison (in absentia) of the former deputy commander of the Crimean unit "Berkut" for exceeding the powers, which caused 20 participants in protest in 2013.

About it is inmessageThe Prosecutor General's Office.

According to the department, the court has imposed a former "Berkut" punishment with deprivation of the right to occupy positions in state and law enforcement agencies for a period of three years. The convicted person is also deprived of the special rank - the lieutenant colonel of the police.

“Prosecutors in court proved that the convicted person, having exceeded his official powers, participated in the suppression of protest shares on December 10-11, 2013, using violence. As a result of such actions, 20 injured injuries were injured, ”the message reads.

The prosecutor's office stated that since March 2014, an ex -law enforcement officer, while in the temporarily occupied territory of the Autonomous Republic of Crimea, provided assistance to representatives of the Russian Federation in conducting blasting activities against Ukraine.

The trial was carried out under the special procedure in ABENTIA, given that the person being hiding in the temporarily occupied territory of Crimea.
