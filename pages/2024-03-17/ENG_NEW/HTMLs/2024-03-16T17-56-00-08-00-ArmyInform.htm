More than 50 states of the United Nations have joined a joint statement with the condemnation of the efforts of the Russian Federation to hold pseudo -election in the temporarily occupied territories of Ukraine.

This was reported by the Permanent Mission of Ukraine to the UN.

"We are most urgently condemn the illegitimate attempts of the Russian Federation to organize the election of the President of Russia to TT within the internationally recognized territory of Ukraine," the statement reads.

It is emphasized that holding elections on the territory of another UN member state without its consent is a blatant disregard for the principles of sovereignty and territorial integrity. Such elections have no legal force under international law.

The statement was supported by Albania, Andorra, Argentina, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Canada, Chile, Costa Rica, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Georgia, Germany. Ireland, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Latvia, Liberia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Marshall Islands, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, New Zealand, Northern Macedonia, Norway, Palai Moldova, Romania, San Marino, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom, USA, Uruguay and the European Union.

The UN countries also recalled the resolution of the General Assembly approved at a special session on October 12, 2022.

She recognized the illegal "referendums" of the Russian Federation in September 2022 in some areas of Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk and Zaporizhzhya regions of Ukraine invalid in terms of international law.
