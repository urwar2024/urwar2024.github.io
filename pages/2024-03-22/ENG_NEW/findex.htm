On March 7, two years after Russia's large -scale invasion of Ukraine forced Sweden to revise its national security policy, it officially joined the North Atlantic Treaty.

During the welcoming procedure, NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg said: “This is a historical day. Sweden will now take her right place at the NATO table with an equal voice regarding the formation of NATO policies and decisions. After over 200 years, Sweden already has the defense of Article 5, a decisive guarantee of freedom and security of the Alliance. Sweden has brought with it capable armed forces and first -class defense industry. Sweden's introduction makes NATO stronger, gives Sweden more security, and the whole alliance makes it better protected. ”

Given the words of NATO Secretary General, the Armyinform gathered information, with which the official Stockholm joined the most powerful political-military bloc.

By its political system, the Kingdom of Sweden is a parliamentary monarchy, which, in turn, has been part of the European Union since 1995, although it continues to use its own currency, the Swedish crown. According to the Central Statistical Administration of Sweden, there are 10 550 054 inhabitants today in the country today. The largest group of foreigners consists of Poles - 56 165 people, followed by Syrians (53 698), Afghans (45 053), Finns (40 227) and Hindus (36 342).

Like Finland, Sweden is a country with a high defensive culture. For many years, it has developed a high -tech its own military industry. Sweden makes everything:

- one -engine planes of Gripen fighters;

- Gotland submarines.

In 2010, Sweden abandoned compulsory military service, but resumed it in 2017 due to the lack of recruits. Now both men and women are called for its implementation. According to Globalfirepower.com, Sweden is ranked 29th in the world military ranking (asreportedThe Armyinform, Ukraine in this plate holds the 18th position in the world and is the fourth strongest army in Europe).

The number of personnel of the Armed Forces is 25.6 thousand people (of which 10.2 thousand are civil workers), including 76% of men and 24% of women, 22.2 thousand servicemen of the National Guard. There are about 5.7 million men and women of conscription (18 to 47 years) in the country. In total, during the first days of the war, Sweden can expose about 64,000 soldiers.
