The Security Service exposed two more organizers of the illegal referendum of the Russian Federation, which took place during the occupation of Right -Bank Kherson region.

About thisShe reported March 21Security Service of Ukraine.

One of the persons was detained in Nikolaev and the other in the Chernivtsi region. They tried to avoid justice under the guise of displaced.

According to the investigation, after the capture of the Kherson region, both figures supported the invaders and became part of the occupation "election committees".

While in the ranks of the pseudo -institution, the collaboration agitated locals "vote for the annexation" of Kherson region to Russia.

Then the 28-year-old resident of Chernobayivka gathered a group of "like-minded people" and recorded a video report on the results of fake plebiscite, which was broadcast on Russian TV channels.

Another malefactor made a double detour on the homes of the villagers and collected "necessary" marks in the "ballots".

It was accompanied by armed invaders who, in the event of people refusing to support the Kremlin's project, threatened with the imprisonment and confiscation of property.

For his cooperation with the aggressor, the person involved received a one -time cash "reward" and the promise of further "prospects" in the occupation institution.

After the release of the region, both collaborators fled to different cities in the south and west of Ukraine, where they tried to "lie down to the bottom".

Based on the evidence collected, the security service reported to the detainees of suspicion under the following articles of the Criminal Code of Ukraine:

  • ⁠ ⁠ch. 2 tbsp. 28, Part 5 of Art. 111-1 (collaborative activity, voluntary occupation by a citizen of Ukraine of a position related to the implementation of organizational-administrative or administrative-economic functions in illegal authorities, committed by prior conspiracy by a group of persons);
  • ⁠ ⁠ch. 1 tbsp. 110 (encroachment on the territorial integrity and inviolability of Ukraine).

The attackers are threatened with up to 10 years in prison.
