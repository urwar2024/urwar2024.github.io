In Kiev, two men were suspected who forged letters from military units and received charitable assistance for UAH 1.7 million.

About thisreportedThe Prosecutor General's Office.

According to the investigation, the suspects, having nothing to do with the Armed Forces, were sent to charitable foundations and volunteer organizations on behalf of military units with a letter of help to receive humanitarian aid.

Representatives of charitable foundations contacted fraudsters by their phone numbers and agreed to provide the necessary assistance.

Subsequently, under the guise of representatives of military units, in military uniform, with seals, allegedly, these units received tactical tools, medical equipment and first aid kits from representatives, which were sold at their own discretion.

At the same time they issued the act of acceptance and transfer of assistance. As evidence of assistance from the fund, the fraudsters even agreed to take a common photo.

The fraud was exposed when representatives of the charitable foundation contacted one of the military units to confirm the fact of receiving assistance, but the military reported that they did not write any letters to the foundation and did not receive assistance.

Currently, the involvement of suspects in seven episodes of fraud totaling about UAH 1.7 million has been involved.

During the searches, the suspects seized about half a million hryvnias, mobile phones, computer equipment, forged documents and seals of military units, humanitarian aid, military form and other material evidence.

Under the procedural leadership of the Podilsky District Prosecutor's Office of the City of Kyiv, men were informed about suspicion of fraud (Part 4 of Article 190 of the Criminal Code of Ukraine). Both were detained. They also chose a pre -trial detention measure.
