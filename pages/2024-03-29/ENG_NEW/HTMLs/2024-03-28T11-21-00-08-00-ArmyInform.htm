Ukrainian servicemen have undergone an intense five-week course in tactical medicine conducted jointly by British Land Forces, Royal Air Force, the Netherlands Army and the Iceland Fire and Rescue Service.

This is stated inmessageBritish embassies in Ukraine.

The course aimed to teach the only standards of assistance to students with different levels of training - from absolute newcomers to experienced physicians.

The training included the study of the trench system, the evacuation of the wounded from the development, as well as the treatment of shrapnel wounds.

The instructors of the British Land Forces, together with partners from the Netherlands and Iceland, conducted training for Ukrainians on various topics, including sorting of wounded, clinical and psychiatric scenarios so that they can save lives on the battlefield.
