Senior Lieutenant of the Armed Forces of Ukraine Yevgeny Lunyak - Doctor of History, Professor of Nizhyn Mykola Gogol State University, Head of the Department of History of Ukraine. He became the Armed Forces immediately after the Revolution of Dignity, defending the Donetsk airport. And from the first day of a large -scale invasion of Russia in Ukraine went to defend the hometown, which was drawn to the Moscow Horde.

Now Yevgeny is fighting in eastern Ukraine. He recently visited the Armyinform and spoke about the experience of a historian who has become a participant in the viral events of the last decade, who will be written in textbooks for more than a hundred years.

- You participated in the Orange Revolution and the Dignity Revolution. Tell us about your experience, motives.

- I remember quite well in 2004, the Orange Revolution. Then I tried to come to the Maidan, wearing orange ribbons. It was a very pleasant time, and most importantly, it was a bloodless revolution. It ended with the victory of the people. And when someone says that we cannot change anything and it is impossible to overthrow the authorities, it makes no sense, then I always answer that in 2004, during the Orange Revolution, the Ukrainians defended the choice of the people - and the president became the one Who received the majority of votes.

As for the Revolution of Dignity, I very much hoped that it would also be bloodless, but unfortunately, this did not happen. He witnessed those events, many times on the Maidan. And, unfortunately, on February 18, 2014, when mass shootings began, I was there too. That day, I realized that we were falling into the turbulence phase. I realized that this would obviously be the war. Well, in fact, it happened when the occupation of Crimea began. As soon as the first "green men" appeared, I told my wife that it was a war.

And immediately in March I went to the military enlistment office-and since March 20, 2014 I served in the 1st tank brigade.

-Your motivation in 2014 and 2022 was different?

- Motivation did not differ because the main motive is the protection of Ukraine. But if in the 14th I did not know what the Ukrainian army was, because I came an absolutely civilian person, then in 2022 there was already more confidence, despite the fact that the situation was much more difficult. There was already some experience. I knew where I went and what to do. Indeed, it was quite important.

I believe that in those conditions we were able to do the maximum to stop the large -scale invasion, so as not to let the enemy move where he wanted.

- Why tank troops?

-In 2014, I could get to any troops. I am from Nizhyn, and the 1st tank brigade is stationed in the Chernihiv region. Since the military assembly was held in those units that were in the regions, we were respectively sent to the meeting to the 1st tank.
