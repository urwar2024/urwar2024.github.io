On March 22, the Government adopted the resolution developed by the Ministry of Social Policy and clarified the list of IDPs that will be entitled to pay for 6 months.

This is said inmessageGovernment portal.

From March 1, 2024, the IDP residence permit was extended for another 6 months for certain IDP categories, which received it for one to two years (at least 8 months).

Priority payments have been continued for people who are unable to work, care for children or seriously ill or disabled persons, have no income or have low income (more detail -at the link).

Now, in order to avoid different readings, the decree clearly stipulates that, among others, they have the right to continue assistance for another 6 months also the following categories of people:

  • persons with severe forms of disease who have not established disability (and families in which such persons reside);
  • children who are arranged in a patronage caregiver; patronage caregivers; children temporarily arranged in a family of relatives, acquaintances, foster care, family -type orphanage;

If the average monthly total income per recipient in the family is less than UAH 9444:

  • persons who care for a child under 3 years or a child in need of care;
  • families with children under 18 and/or up to 23 years of age, who are full-time or dual education students in vocational (vocational), professional and higher education;
  • persons who provide social services on a non -professional basis;
  • students who go up to 23 years of age;
  • persons with disabilities of group III;
  • Families of pensioners.

In addition, persons older than 55 years have also received the right to continue assistance for an additional 6 months.

The period of submission of payments for IDPs by April 30, 2024 was also extended. At the same time, even those who will submit applications in April will receive funds based on March.

If a person falls under these criteria and submits an application in May, then the assistance will be assigned from the month of appeal, ie also from May.

The government also noted that within two months of assistance, IDPs who do not work and do not care for children or persons with disabilities should promote their employment.

That is, to register at the Employment Center (or register as an individual entrepreneur and began the relevant activity/ or receive assistance for economic independence/ micro-grant to create or develop your own business/ or get a voucher for study, etc.).

If after the assistance, the person will not do so, the payment of assistance will be stopped.
