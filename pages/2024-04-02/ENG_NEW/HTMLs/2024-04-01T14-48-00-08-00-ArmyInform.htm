Nearly 100 Ukrainians were victims of four malefactors who cheated citizens under the guise of charitable organizations from Poland.

About thisreported on April 1Cyber Police.

The defendants found on the Internet an announcement of fundraising to assist the Armed Forces and treatment of the military.

By giving themselves for representatives of international volunteer organizations or payment institutions, frauds from fake pages on social networks offered "assistance".

To transfer funds, as a rule from Poland in PLN, the attackers suggested that the victims take some actions allegedly to convert funds.

In fact, the scammers launched personal and bank data, which included the victims of the Internet banking and withdrew money for bank accounts.

Last summer, police officers conducted a series of searches for residence and cars of malefactors.

Mobile phones, 300 seven cards, computer equipment, bank cards, rough records, cash in different currency for a total amount of almost 800 thousand UAH, as well as 4 cars have been seized.

Investigators found that the accused had taken over almost 100 residents from different regions of Ukraine. The total amount of losses is UAH 1.3 million.

Currently, all members of the criminal group are in custody. Law enforcement officers completed the investigation and sent an indictment to court. They face up to eight years in prison.

Asreported the armyinform, fraudsters began to use fake petitions about the title of Hero of Ukraine.
