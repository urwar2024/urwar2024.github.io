Council of Europe Commissioner for Human Rights Michael O'Flaherty, who begins his work on April 1, announced his visit to Ukraine.

About thisIt is saidIn his statement, posted on the official website of the Council of Europe.

According to the department, today Michael O'Flaertie begins his six-year mandate and promises to put human rights in the center of the agenda of the Council of Europe.

He noted that his first visit to the commissioner, according to his election promises, would make it in Ukraine.

"I can't think of anything more urgent than addressing the human rights implications of Russia's large-scale invasion of Ukraine, which has caused terrible human suffering and destruction," he said.

The commissioner thanked his predecessor Duny Miyatovich and emphasized his intention to focus on youth, socio-economic rights and human rights defenders, as well as use his mandate to promote the enforcement of decisions of the European Court of Human Rights.
