Publication of personal data of prisoners of war or missing Ukrainians on social networks, as well as their transfer to third parties may harm them and their families.

About thiswarnsThe Ministry of Reintegration, noting that the Russian special services intensified the collection of personal data of missing Ukrainians.

“Numerous telegram channels promise to find servicemen who have been captured or disappeared. To do this, they ask for relatives and close personal data and other information, ”the department emphasized.

So what information cannot be transmitted to strangers:

• phone number, other contacts.

• residence address.

• information about political views, previous place of work, family of captives.

• profile on social networks.

• Data of a serviceman (name, brigade number, military specialty, call sign, title, circumstances of captivity).

• the location of the unit.

• Photo/video, including with brothers.

Why don't you should do this

"By passing information to outsiders, you can worsen your loved one," - added to the Ministry of Reintegration.

To date, representatives of the intelligence services of the aggressor or fraudsters organize many channels for the purpose of denying money. The invaders can also resort to manipulation or blackmail.

"Remember that your loved one is engaged in the search for professionally and free of charge by the state authorities," - said the Ministry ofrahitegration.
