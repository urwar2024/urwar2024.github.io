Colonel Natalia Humeniuk said that the Russian leadership is looking for a new commander for her fleet to find creative solutions to change the situation.

Natalia Humeniuk provided her comments during the March 12 2024 on the morning of March 12, 2024.

"Obviously, they are now conducting a personnel reformatting and the search for such a leader who will find a creative decision for the further development of naval events,"-said the spokeswoman for the South Defense Forces.

According to her, the forces of defense will calm down early, because the enemy still has rocket launchers.

"We understand that the enemy has such a powerful element as rocket carriers, although they are somewhat limited in the sense of use because the next service is complicated - it is on the coast of Crimea, and there the enemy is very dangerous," Natalia Humeniuk said.

The fact that the Russian command put on a pause the fighting of ships in the Black Sea, it also explained the re -equipment of enemy base points.

“And the fact that they are being reconstructed at the same time, it also complicates their current combat work. That is why we see a certain pause in their stay, ”the spokeswoman said.

At the same time, Natalia Humeniuk emphasized at a high level of rocket danger.

“But we continue to evaluate the level of rocket danger from the sea as extremely high. I will remind that there are rocket launchers there, about 10 units, and their equipment can be in several dozen "caliber" missiles. And a certain degree of despair of the enemy can lead to the fact that they can be applied and directly from the bay. Therefore, air alarm signals are always a warning of danger. They should not be ignored, ”the spokeswoman for the south defense forces summarized.

On March 10, 2024, the media reported that the Commander-in-Chief of the Naval Fleet of Russia, Admiral Mykola Yevmenov, was resigned. Admiral Alexander Moiseev is allegedly replaced.

As reportedArmyinform, the Navy of the Armed Forces indicated a unique pause with the removal of enemy ships into the Black Sea.
