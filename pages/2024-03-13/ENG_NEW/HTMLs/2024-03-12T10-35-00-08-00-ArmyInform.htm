Thales defense electronic systems with headquarters in Paris reported that it has successfully delivered the first CAPTAS-4 hydraulic acoustic device with a variable depth for the Constel Frigate Program (FFG-62) US Navy.

From the general information posted on the portal of the manufacturerknownthat the Thales International Industrial Group has developed CAPTAS-4 marine drones to combat submarines at depths, notes the army.

CAPTAS-4 work as echolotos/sons, giving out the location of submarines to warships, as well as charges.

Thales Vice Squad on Strategic Development Mark Bok, which previously served as an officer in the US Navy, revealed the details of this contract and the value of the equipment itself ininterviewDefense News profile portal.

The expert recalled that during the Cold War, the military searched for submarines with the help of "passive sonar" - highly sensitive equipment that captures sounds. Later, quieter submarines appeared, so they began to be caught by echolocation.

"CAPTAS-4 uses a similar principle-the device can be dropped from the ship's feed and lowered to a depth of 15 to 180 meters, the most effective for the search for submarines. Once in a given position, the equipment begins to produce sound waves that are reflected from the objects and determine their location, and then the data is transferred to the command-commanding point, ”the expert said.

Mark Bok highlighted two main advantages of the system - the great action radius and distribution of signals. Since the CAPTAS-4 "noise" in the water, the submarine can hear and dump it and then attack it. However, the signal of the enemy's location will not receive a drone, but other accompanying echo of the vessel, which will not be known to be hostile submarine.

“This is a very scary scenario for the submarines. And the United States should be proud of that they will have such an advantage in defense, ”the Thales representative summarized.

According to Mark Bok, the CAPTAS-4 contract will be adapted for use on US military frigates of the Constel (FFG-62).

“If there is at least one area in which French defensive know-how has received a general recognition, then it is a Protetic War. Whether these are frigates, submarines, or marine patrols, the French fleet very often demonstrated advanced technical capabilities in profile combat work, even exceeding such very effective allies - America and the United Kingdom. Thus, in recent years, new French frigates of the Aquitania class have repeatedly won the Hook'EM contest, organized by the US Navy, which identifies the best NATO crews in the field of anti -water fighting underwater, ” - not without boastingDescribedCAPTAS-4 device French portal Meta-Defense.fr.
