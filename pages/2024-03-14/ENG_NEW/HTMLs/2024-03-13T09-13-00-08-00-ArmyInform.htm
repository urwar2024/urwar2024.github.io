The European Commission has submitted to the European Council proposals for the negotiation framework for Ukraine and Moldova and will provide an oral report on the progress of both countries on the way to European integration.

About thisIt is saidOn the European Commission's website.

It is noted that these framework establishes rules and principles of negotiation on accession with each candidate country. The EC also announced plans to provide the Council's verbal report on the progress reached by both countries on the implementation of the recommendations provided by the Commission in the Extension Report of November 8.

“The project of the framework prepared by the European Commission consists of three parts:

  1. principles governing negotiations;
  2. the essence of negotiations;
  3. Negotiation procedure.

The purpose of the negotiations is to adoption Ukraine and the Moldova normative framework of the EU in full and ensure its full implementation and implementation, ”the message reads.

The project of the negotiation framework is based on the experience of past extensions and current negotiations.

According to the European Commission, the European Council should continue to initiate the internal discussion of these texts. After the Euro Council approves the negotiating framework, the Council presiding the EU Council will present an agreed EU joint position at the first intergovernmental conference with each country that will commemorate the official start of entry negotiations. At this stage, the negotiation frames will be made public.

Foreign Minister of Ukraine Dmytro KulebagreetedThe European Commission on the Project of the Framework Agreement with Ukraine, which will be represented by the Council, has been revised.

"The European Union is gradually moving towards its natural political borders. In the current conditions, this is a completely reasonable strategy that will ensure long-term peaceful and democratic development for all Europeans and EU member states," the Foreign Minister wrote on Twitter.
