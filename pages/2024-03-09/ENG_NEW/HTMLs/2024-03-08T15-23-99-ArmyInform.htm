The Estonian Ministry of Defense has stated that all products of manufacturers who continue to work in Russia will be removed from outlets on the territory of the Estonia Defense Forces and other places subordinate to the department.

About thisinformsEstonia's defense agency.

It is noted that the goods from manufacturers who continue to work in Russia and thus contribute to the financing of the Russian military machine will be removed from the outlets on the territory of the Estonia forces and other places subordinated to the Ministry of Defense.

The decision concerns the manufacturers who have included the List of International Sponsors of War. In trade vending machines, cafes and other retail outlets, the Ministry of Defense will no longer sell products of companies such as Pepsico, Mars, Nestle, Unilever and Mondelēz International. This includes chocolate, cookies, chips, ice cream, erasers and soft drinks.

"Many Western companies have ceased their operations in Russia, but there are still those who support the Russian economy and, accordingly, its military machine," said Defense Minister Hanno Picur.

Also stopped distribution of tobacco companies Philip Morris International and Japan Tobacco International (JTI).

Hanno, Makur, reminds that most goods are also alternatives from domestic manufacturers, and that the idea of a solution is greater than the small inconvenience caused by the situation.
