The US Department of Defense has concluded a $ 3.4 billion contract with Boeing for the supply of P-8A Poseidon Maritime Patrolling aircraft Germany and Canada.

Such data derive fromreportPentagon, published on the website of the Defense Office of the United States, reports the Armyinform.

According to the document, 14 Boeing P-8A Poseidon aircraft will be delivered to Canada, three more will be sent to Germany, and the agreement includes accompanying engineering work. Full order execution is expected in March 2028.

Most production capacity (98%) will be used in Seattle, Washington, and a number of enterprises in California, Arizona and Alabami.

"The Boeing Corporation has received a fixed price of $ 3 407 940 833 for the purchase and delivery of 17 multi-purpose P-8A ships," the publication said.

The contractor is the command of the Pataxent River, Maryland, Maryland. Boeing, according to the materials of the US Defense Ministry, will also develop software for the training systems of flight crews and maintenance of F/A-18E fighter fighters and EA-18G aircraft for Australia.

According to the US Department of Defense, the Boeing contract is part of the US strategy to strengthen NATO's cooperation with NATO allies on security in Atlantic and Pacific regions. He also noted that the supply of P-8A Poseidon aircraft helps to increase the ability of Canada and Germany to counteract threats.

Reference:Boeing P-8 Poseidon (Poseidon-in ancient Greek mythology, the Supreme Sea God. Created on the basis of the passenger liner Boeing 737-800. It is designed to identify and destroy the enemy submarines in patrol areas, as well as for intelligence, participation in anti -ship and rescue operations - both in coastal areas and in the oceans.

Boeing P-8 POSEIDON can carry up to nine tons of weapons, including hydroacoustic buoys designed to detect submarine at great depths, a multi-purpose search radar. It has five internal and six external pendants, for different types of weapons - torpedoes, sea mines, airbags.

The P-8A Poseidon aircraft first took off in 2009. Since 2013, these aircraft are part of the US Navy, as well as the Navy of Australia, India, the United Kingdom and Norway. Canada and Germany will be the sixth and seventh buyers of the Poseidon aircraft respectively.

In November 2023, the Government of Canada announced the order of the P-8A Poseidon to replace the current park of outdated CP-140 Aurora (Canadian P-3 ORION version), which has been in service for over 40 years. The US State Department approved the sale of these aircraft and related equipment last June.

For investments in the Project, the purchase of new patrols and related equipment, training devices and equipment for Canada is planning a total of $ 10.4 billion, which is $ 7.7 billion.

The first P-8A for Canada should be arrived in 2026. In this case, all patrol planes are expected to be delivered by the fall of 2027. The complete prompt readiness of all Canadian Poseidon is expected by 2033.

In turn, after the initial purchase of the five P-8A Poseidon in June 2021, Germany decided to order three more additional patrols in November 2023 to reach the total number of P-8 for the Navy of Germany. The first aircraft will be delivered in 2025.

New American patrol planes will replace the fleet of profile turboperous P-3C Orion, which are now operated by the German military.

In addition, the German industry is an important partner of the P-8A Poseidon. In Germany, Boeing collaborates with ESG Elektroniksystem-Und Logistik-GmbH and Lufthansa Technik to provide system integration, training and support for new aircraft.

At the end of 2023, Germany had five such aircraft, and Canada will be the first airliners of this model. Most similar machines in the United States, 100 aircraft, another 28 are in production.
