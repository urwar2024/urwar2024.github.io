The commander of the multinational educational operation of Interflex Colonel James Turkan spoke about the experience of conducting basic general -military training of personnel of the Armed Forces of Ukraine.

This is stated in themessageGeneral Staff of the Armed Forces of Ukraine.

James Turchan spoke with the topic to the group of official military-diplomatic representatives of the partner countries whose embassies are located in London.

The visit of a group of military attaches took place at one of the training grounds, where the Interflex operation is prepared.

James Turchan, in particular, told about the uniqueness and features of how the Interflex operation was organized.

"Military attachés had the opportunity to communicate both with foreign instructors conducting classes and with representatives of the Ukrainian army who are undergoing training," the statement said.

The Interflex Operation is a curriculum that is intended for recruits of the Armed Forces of Ukraine with small or without military experience.

More than 35K Ukrainian servicemen and servicewomen have already been trained in the implementation of the multinational training operation INTERFLEX in 2023 with the participation of the best instructors of the Armed Forces of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, the Commonwealth of Australia, the Kingdom of Denmark, Canada, the Republic of Estonia, the Republic of Lithuania, the Kingdom of the Netherlands, Romania, New Zealand, the Kingdom of Norway, Finland, the Kingdom of Sweden and Ukraine of the Armed Forces of Ukraine.

The training is conducted on the objects of the training and material base of the UK Ministry of Defense. Each training course lasts five weeks.

As reported by the Armyinform, earlier in March 2024 James Turkantalkedwith instructors of the UK Armed Forces and servicemen of the Armed Forces of Ukraine, who are trained in the framework of the Interflex operation, in particular regarding the development of assault actions in the area with suburban development.
