Saab AB has announced that the Swedish Defense Materials Authority (FMV) has submitted an application for the conceptual design of future fighter jets for the country's Air Force.

ЯкindicateThe authors of the Meta-Defense portal, defensive studies will be carried out within the framework of the FlyGvapnet of the Swedish Air Force. Even the working name of the future aircraft is not yet given, reports the army.

The Swedish company's press service reported that it is currently considering three ways, one of which will lead to the selection of a multi-purpose Jas-39E/F Gripen Multiple Fighter.

In this case, the work will include the study of the possibility of combining in one system of manned and unmanned elements, creating concepts, as well as working out the future production process. To achieve this, SAAB will cooperate with FMV, Swedish armed forces, FII Swedish Agency, GKN Aerospace and other industrial partners.

“Saab is in a strong position and thanks to the recent development of Gripen E andGlobalEye, we have advanced technologies and engineering know-how necessary to promote the concept of the fighter of the future. This means that we will continue to offer innovative solutions to meet the future operational needs of the Swedish Armed Forces and other customers, ”Zona Militar says the words of the SAAB Director of Larsman Aeronautics Director of SAAB.

It is noted that the famous Swedish aircraft manufacturer intends to continue to develop cooperation with interested parties working on new and breakthrough technologies that will play an important role in promising combat aircraft systems. SAAB will also study innovative platforms, including the "Initiative of Defense Innovation" of the Sweden Government and the NATO Project "Defense Innovation accelerator for the North Atlantic".

Conceptual studies within the concluded contract will be conducted in parallel with the modernization of Jas-39C/D Gripen aircraft and the adoption of the Armed Forces of Sweden and Brazil Gripen E.

GKN Aerospace will conduct conceptual research on power plants for promising fighters. They are expected to help create the basis for promising fighter systems and continue to create similar solutions in Sweden.

The term of the contract for the development of the concept of the future aircraft is designed for 2024-2025.

Defense market analysts emphasize that, in fact, with this contract, the Swedish authorities, who know that they are closely monitored by representatives of the FCAS aviation program (Germany, Spain, France and Belgium) and the GCAP project (Italy, Japan and the United Kingdom), want to have a global and objective vision of the situation in the world in order to simultaneously meet their future needs for specialized aviation and maintain their production skills.
