The United States of America has announced new sanctions against a network that helped finance missile production in Iran.

About thisinformsUS Ministry of Finance.

In particular, the US Treasury Department imposed restrictions on three procurement networks based in Iran, Turkey, Oman and Germany. They supported Iran's ballistic missile, nuclear, and defense programs.

These networks have purchased carbon fiber, epoxy resins and other missile products used by the Iranian Organization of Aerospace forces of the Corps of the Guardians of the Islamic Revolution, the Ministry of Defense and Logistics of the Iran Armed Forces and other organizations against which sanctions have already been imposed.

The Deputy Minister of Finance for the Combating Terrorism and Financial Intelligence Brian Nelson said that with the help of complex secret procurement networks, Iran seeks to supply fraudsters around the world of weapons that ignite conflicts and risk the innumerable lives of civilians.

So the United States imposed sanctions against:

  • Citizen Iran and Germany Masiara Karima;
  • German companies Mazixon GmbH and Mazixon Verwaltungs;
  • Omani companies MAA;
  • Turkish citizen Mahmut Gokok;
  • Turkish companies Uluslararasi, Gokler, Skies Petroleum, DM Gold and Klas Kimasal.

Rostam Shahmari, Goce Biklo, Piszro Mobtaker Paywand and Mitra Inanlu were also subject to restrictions, but the Ministry of Finance did not specify their citizenship.
