The US Air Force announced the fifth test launch of a full-fledged prototype of the AGM-183 ARRW air-to-ground hypersonic missile.

According to the information,commonReuters, current combat tests that took place in the Pacific Ocean are recognized as successful against the backdrop of previous tests that have failed, reports the Armyinform.

Although the US Air Force does not disclose the speed of flight of the latest weapons, during the preliminary tests of the ARRW, better known as Air-Launched Rapid Responsse Weapon (Aviation Weapons of Rapid Response. sound.

In a comment for Defense News profile portal a representative of the US Air Forcesaidthat the rocket was launched from the American bomber B-52h StratoFortress after take-off from Andersen's airbase on Guami.

The test was conducted at the Reagan landfill, an army site in the Marshall Islands.

The Air Force has refused to name the entire tests, but stated that they "received valuable information about opportunities" of hypersonic weapons production of Lockheed Martin.

The AGM-183A Arrw rocket is created in response to similar developments in Russia and China. However, as repeatedlyreportedArmyInform, all the statements of the Kremlin administration about the creation of hypersonic "superweapons" are reduced to the partial modernization of previous models. And the video demonstrations of defense innovations presented by Putin back in 2019 remained "cartoons".

Meanwhile, the Pentagon expects that the B-52H StratoFortress (four missiles per plane) will receive the first hypersonic missile. In the future, it is planned to be adopted by promising strategic bombers B-21 Raider.

Reference:The AGM-183 ARRW is an air-launched ballistic missile equipped with a hypersonic glide unit. The Air-Launched Rapid Response Weapon is one of the United States Air Force's two major programs to develop air-launched hypersonic weapons.
