The Polish Government has doubled its obligations within the Czech Republic headed by the initiative to purchase hundreds of thousands of artillery shells for Ukraine.

This was stated by Polish Foreign Minister Radoslav Sikorsky, reportsBloomberg.

Radoslav Sikorsky spoke to Riga journalists with his Latvian counterpart Krishianis Karinsh, but refused to name a specific amount of funding.

The Czech Plan envisages the purchase of ammunition outside the European Union and the delivery of 800,000 shells in Ukraine.

Asreported the armyinform, in the Czech Republic, Ukraine will receive the first shells within the Czech initiative in June.

In addition, the Czech Foreign Minister Jan Lipavskynamed the figure of 1.5 million shellsas a potential volume that can be set as part of an AFA Initiative to provide ammunition.
