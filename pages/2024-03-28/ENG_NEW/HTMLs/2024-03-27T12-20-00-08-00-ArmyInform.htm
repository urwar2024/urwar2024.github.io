The Security Service of Ukraine prevented the escape of the suspect in cooperation with the Russian occupation troops.

This is stated inmessageSBU as well as inmessageThe Prosecutor General's Office.

"During the complex events in Kherson, a local resident was detained, who protected the Russian torture during the occupation of the city," the statement said.

According to the investigation, during the release of Kherson, the suspect burned hostile to the "prison warden certificate" and moved to the rented apartment to "replace the tracks".

In this way, he planned, as noted by the SBU, to be hidden by January 2025, when he was 60 years old and then left abroad as a displaced from south of Ukraine.

"However, the collaborator did not wait for this - the SBU staff searched him and detained it in temporary premises," the statement said.

During a search in the detainee's house, law enforcement officers found a mobile phone with evidence of criminal acts, as well as uniforms of Russian sample clothing, which, according to the investigation, was given the suspect by Russian invaders.

According to the investigation, at the beginning of a large-scale invasion of the Russian Federation, the suspect worked as an engineer-electromechanic at a local enterprise.

"But after the capture of Kherson, the man voluntarily joined the" administration "of the Russian prison, which operated in the captured local correctional colony at that time," the SBU notes.

He was appointed "junior inspector of the supervision and safety department" of the occupation institution.

In this "position" he guarded cameras in which the prisoners of the resistance movement in the region were kept.

"The Rashists tortured Ukrainian patriots and tried to" knock out "them consent to cooperate with the aggressor," the SBU notes.

The detainee has now reported suspicion under Part 7 of Art. 111-1 of the Criminal Code of Ukraine (collaborative activity, voluntary occupation by a citizen of Ukraine in illegal law enforcement agencies established in the temporarily occupied territory).

He is in custody. He faces up to 15 years in prison with confiscation of property.
