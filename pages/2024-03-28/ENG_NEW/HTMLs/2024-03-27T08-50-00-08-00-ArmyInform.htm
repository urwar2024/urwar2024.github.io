The UN Monitoring Mission has received proven reports on the part of the Russian invaders at least 32 Ukrainian prisoners of war during this winter. This is much more than in previous periods.

About thisIt is saidIn the report of the UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission in Ukraine (MMPL).

This is the 38th UN mission report on human rights situation in Ukraine, which covers the period from December 1, 2023 to February 29, 2024. Experts interviewed 60 Ukrainian military, recently released from captivity.

"Almost every Ukrainian prisoners of war surveyed told how Russian servicemen or officials tortured them during their captivity, using repeated beatings, electric shocks, threats of execution, long -term stress poses and imitation of the shooting," said MMPL head Daniel Bell.

The report provided reliable reports "at least 32 Ukrainian prisoners of war in 12 separate cases in the period from December 2023 to February 2024, which is much more than in any of the previous periods."

"The IMPLU conducted an independent verification of three of the mentioned cases," the report reads.

The UN also provides interview data with 44 Russian prisoners of war, who, although not declaring torture in official places of detention, gave "torture certificates and abuse in places of their temporary stay after evacuation from the battlefield."

In addition, the mission report contains information on the violence of the Russian administration against the residents of the occupied territories of Ukraine, in particular, it is about the murder of civilians, arbitrary detentions and restriction of freedom of expression.

As reportedArmyinform, more than 10 thousand peaceful Ukrainians are kept by Russians in the occupied territories and in the Russian Federation.
