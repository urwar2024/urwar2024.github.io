Our patient with a triple amputation independently made repair at home - Major of Medical Service Olga Shcheglyuk

Author: Army Inform
Categories: Life story, Військова медицина, Новини
Description: Since 2018, Olga has been working with military people who have lost their limbs during combat missions ....
Published Time: 2024-03-27T06:34:00+08:00

Major of Medical Service Olga Shcheglyuk is a neurologist, the head of the rehabilitation department of servicemen with amputated limbs in the military-medical clinical rehabilitation center in Irpen.

Since 2018, Olga has been working with military people who have lost their limbs during combat missions. Side by side with the rehabilitation team put many patients on their feet, some of them continue to protect Ukraine and destroy the enemy in the hottest directions.

With the beginning of a large -scale invasion, Major Shcheglyuk works in the ward, which deals with particularly complex cases of double and triple amputations. Some of her patients have experienced four amputations - and continues to adapt to a full life.

The Armyinform spoke with a medical doctor about her difficult but essential profession in a country that is in a large -scale war.

“Anxious backpack was made only for a half -month -old child”

Medicine is my choice from conscious age, from ninth grade. I studied in the natural lyceum with a biological direction. Further - six years of Bogomolets Medical University. In 2016, she signed a contract and joined the Ukrainian Military Medical Academy (UVMA). In two years she graduated from UVMA with a gold medal, received a master's degree with honors in the specialty "Internal Diseases". She was distributed to the Military Medical Clinical Rehabilitation Center of Irpin.

But even working - I continue to study, because my doctor's training never ends with a diploma: I constantly undergo trainings, conferences, courses, internships, monitor the upgrading of protocols, recommendations. Last year, specialization in physical and rehabilitation medicine.

While studying at the university, I did not think about rehab. But she realized that it was important, because 2014 showed everything: people began to return from the war, and we did not have enough specialists or equipment.

I realized that it was important, although in the future I saw in therapy or cardiology, I liked these areas. And only when she got into rehab, did she understand how close this field of medicine is.

Did I expect the invasion of the Russians on February 24, 2022? .. Logically the brain understood that ... illogical to make a large -scale invasion.

Personally, I had no anxious bag. At the beginning of a large -scale invasion, I was just on maternity leave with a half -month -old son. Therefore, on February 22 or 23, they made an alarming backpack for the baby. Just in case. For themselves, the documents have always been at hand.

On the morning of February 24, we woke up from the explosions, but that day we did not leave anywhere: there were congestion everywhere. On February 25, the man took us with a child to the west of Ukraine, and the next day he returned to Kiev and reconciled again for military service.

While in the event, I advised online for some time as a doctor, engaged in training projects, participated in the creation of training courses on rehabilitation of patients with amputated limbs together from Protez Hub and so on.

I understood that patients with amputations would become larger and more. And the question of their rehabilitation will be very acute in the near future and will affect each hospital. At the beginning of 2022, the rehabilitation of patients with amputations and their prosthetics was engaged in a limited number of institutions and a limited number of specialists, and it was clear that it would not be enough, that everyone would face it.

“A lot depends on rehabilitation: even the best prosthesis can just be an expensive device”

At the beginning of a large -scale invasion, I remember, I read Linchevsky, who said that every hospital would deal with a military injury and everyone should be able to assist. So it happened. Now the country is engaged in rehabilitation of our military.

Last year, when my son was 1 year and 3 months, I left maternity leave. But there was a desire to come out even earlier, though the man was restrained: the baby needed my attention, and there was no one to leave it.

… The quality of rehabilitation depends a lot. The further life of the wounded fighter depends on how qualitatively the assistance will be provided; It depends on whether he returns to society normally, whether he can find a job, be socially active, or maybe return to service.

Because even if there is the best prosthesis with the best details, but the fighter is not trained to walk and use it and has not been prepared for prosthesis, this prosthesis will be just an expensive device that will stand somewhere in the corner in the room.

We start early rehab with patients when they are still seams. We begin the preparation, formation of stump, involve traumatologists as needed. The patient has a multidisciplinary team that develops an individual rehabilitation plan. At the stage when the stump is ready, the patient chooses a prosthetic enterprise, which we have a lot.

When the prosthetics begins, we accompany the patient in this process: training, walking, balance training, skin hygiene and more. First walking - in bars, in rooms, then - outside.

It should be understood that military injury is much more complicated than simply, for example, amputation due to some disease. Because there can be several amputations, visual impairment, spinal, traumatic brain injury, fractures of other extremities, etc. Therefore, a compulsory multidisciplinary approach to rehabilitation, when each specialist knows their tasks clearly, communicates with others, involves other specialists when needed. The process should be complex and holistic.

“Patient with four amputations a week after the start of rehabilitation was able to eat alone”

We had a patient, 40 years old, with four amputations after captivity. He had frostbite. The stumps were in a terrible state and were not suitable for prosthetics at the moment when it came to us. Traumatologists re -remained him and formed the stumps again. The physical therapist began to work with the patient at the acute stage in the surgery. When the wounds healed and the condition was more or less stable, it was transferred to us into rehabilitation, and we started a comprehensive work with him.

Initially, the patient was very addicted to the help of outsiders: he could not serve himself, neither eat nor transfer. He was constantly with his mother or sister who helped everything.

When our physical therapist Marina Mikhailovska began to work with him, the patient began to perform basic daily activities, such as special devices within a week; Clean your teeth, eat yourself, move from bed to the wheel chair and vice versa.

Often the class began with the question that he needed at this moment, what task he wanted to accomplish. He said that, for example, put the phone on charging. And our physical therapist helped him to develop this skill.

It would seem that the usual action for us is to charge the phone. And for some of our patients, it is difficulty: to take something, to sit, to eat. We prepared this patient for prosthetics, he was minimally dependent on the assistance of outsiders.

Approximately three months after the start of rehabilitation, it became completely mobile, as far as possible in its condition, could even move from the wheel chair to the floor and vice versa. It is now prosthetized in Superhumans.

Prosthetics for him is much more complicated, as it is necessary to prosthetize the upper and lower extremities immediately so that he can keep the balance better, wear prostheses and more. All this should be done in the complex.

“Patient with three amputations skiing and making repairs at home”

Yes, many of our patients participated in sports competitions - both Invictus Games, Warriors Games, and in the US Marine Corps. They got a lot of medals. It motivates them and everyone else.

Our patients are discharged from our institution - but never disappear from the field of view. They are constantly reminded of themselves, sometimes they just send photos, share plans, come to our new military with amputations, communicate with them, tell about their experience and inspire them. "Equal - equal" is a very cool approach in rehabilitation, when the mentor is the same person with amputation as you - a person who has passed a similar path.

For example, our center recently visited a patient, a veteran with a triple amputation, which was undergoing rehab. He has a multi -level amputation: the lower extremity - at the level of the thigh, the other lower extremity - at the level of the lower leg, the upper extremity - at the level of the forearm. And the prosthesis of the eye. That is, he sees only one eye and operates only one upper extremity.

After the rehabilitation and prosthetics, he married, adopted three children, skiing, independently repaired at home. And recently his son was born. It has been on prostheses for 7 years. When he came to us in the hospital, he already helped other patients to exercise with prostheses and shared Lifehacks. And we saw a shock in the eyes of other people from how he did it all: how quickly he removed and wore them, how quickly he crossed the wheel chair on the bed, the floor and vice versa - without prostheses, how many nuances he knew about prostheses.

Very often, such moments are broken in many people's heads.

The success of our patients is, of course, their merit. For this, I, in fact, love my work, because nothing motivates as when you see how they return to civilian life or to service. I am incredibly proud of those who reckled again in 2022. And there were so many, and, unfortunately, some of them have already died. Someone continues the service: not just in the rear positions, but 24/7 on the forefront. And these are people who have been in the war since 2014.

“Sport is a versatile rehab tool”

Sport is a supermegacrute thing in rehabilitation. Yes, it is not suitable for everyone. But for most, for long -term rehabilitation, it is an incredible thing.

Because physical activity not only helps a person to keep himself in shape, but is also a great way to combat post-traumatic stress disorder, depression, anxiety, overcoming the consequences of traumatic brain injuries. Sport is a versatile rehab tool, if you approach it right, under the guidance of a trainer.

Adaptive sports must be developed in Ukraine and should receive greater support from the state. There should be regional networks where veterans would be able to engage.

And here is not only about patients with amputations, but also people who did not have visible injuries, or those who suffered mild traumatic brain injuries or fractures. We do not see injuries visually, but the psychological condition of a person who has experienced everything can be much more difficult than a person with amputations, for example.

In Britain, there is an interesting study that they conducted among their patients with amputations and those patients who have suffered mild injuries. The result was unexpected: after the rehabilitation was completed, the psychological condition was the best in patients with amputations. This was, in particular, due to the fact that they have a lot of reintegration programs, sports opportunities, psychological programs, and they are very focused on the attention and respect of society. But, unfortunately, it is unknown how the situation will change, say, in 10-20 years.

Thanks to their titanic work, we now have a specialty "Physical and Rehabilitation Medicine" in Ukraine, which did not exist until 2019.

Yes, we have something else to work in the field of rehabilitation. The network of prosthetics facilities has increased very quickly. Many new ones have emerged.

Many new prosthetics technologies have come. Unfortunately, we still have a lack of prosthesis. Many of them need advanced training, improvement of knowledge and skills. I do not love this phrase very much, but in fact the war has become a kind of engine of progress, in particular, of course, in medicine.

And to voice it is very difficult, because the patient will still look for ways to solve and those specialists who promise him the ability to walk on prostheses. But I understand that it will still be disappointed at the end. And this is the most painful.

And the most interesting thing in my profession and what I am inspired is the motivation of my patients, their ability to fight to achieve goals. What they do not give up. Finding a vacancy and choosing a position for service in the Armed Forces of the Armed Forces under a contract or official mobilization before the end of martial law, it is advisable to prepare a package of documents for the CCC.

Some of them will be filled in place in the established form, and others should be taken care of in advance.

Recommendation letter

It is worth starting with a recommendation letter from the commander of the military unit where you plan to serve. You can get it after an interview if your education, professional knowledge and experience meet the requirements for replacing your vacant position.

After agreeing on both parties, the candidate is provided with a letter of recommendation, which is also called written consent or agreement from the unit.

It is not necessary to have this document before contacting the CPC for contract service or official mobilization. However, its availability is extremely desirable, as it guarantees that the shopping center of the joint venture or training center will send you to pass the service to the military unit and to the positions specified in the letter of recommendation.

According toexplanationsThe Ministry of Defense on the new recruitment algorithm to the Armed Forces, the Labor Code and the training centers are obliged to send recruits for service to military units of priority staffing strictly according to the letters provided.

The form of writing a letter of recommendation will be taken care of in the military unit (unit), but it is worth paying attention to the fact that it presents data on your education, previous work experience, which should meet the requirements for replacement of the vacant position, and information about the service in the Armed Forces of Ukraine ( If you have passed it before).

Autobiography

Unlike the letter of recommendation that you should provide for the military unit in which you plan to serve, your autobiography should be prepared yourself. And it should be done in two variants - printed and handwritten. The text of the document, of course, in both cases will be identical.

The autobiography is in an arbitrary form, but with the obligatory guidance of the following information:

  • Full name, first name and patronymic
  • Date and place of birth
  • Education (starting from school) indicating the time of study in the respective institutions and/or the time of their completion
  • Previous places (if any) indicating the position and term of stay on it
  • Place of residence registration and (if necessary) actual stay
  • Contact phone number
  • Information about family members (indicating their full names, names and patronymic, year of birth, place of work and position (if any) and place of residence).

At the end of the autobiography, it is indicated whether you or your close relatives have been prosecuted and whether they are registered in the law enforcement agencies.

The autobiography is certified by a personal signature with the decoding of the name.

Characteristics (Summary) from a previous place of work or training

You should also take care of the characteristics or resumes from the previous place of work or study. This document is also concluded in an arbitrary form with the obligatory guidance of the following information:

  • Last name, name and patronymic person who is characterized (resume)
  • Name of place of work or study
  • Work time (training) at the enterprise (firm, institution, institution, etc.)
  • Position (posts) you occupied while working
  • Professional experience, professional knowledge, skills and skills
  • Personal qualities that influence the performance of professional job responsibilities and establishing relationships in the workforce
  • Data on premiums and awards (reprimands or charges) received during work (training)

The characteristics should be addressed in advance at the place of work or study, as the preparation of this document may take some time. Most likely, your appeal will not be the first, because the practice of concluding such documents is quite widespread, so they will conclude documents according to a known established scheme.

The characteristics (resume) of the head (responsible person) and the seal at the place of previous work (training) are certified. This should also be done with a copy of the workbook that will be discussed below.

Documents

  • A copy of the passport (ID-passport) of a citizen of Ukraine
  • A copy of the taxpayer card (certificate of assignment of identification number)
  • A copy of the birth document
  • Copies of Education Documents
  • Copy of the Military Accounting Document (military ticket or attributed certificate)
  • A copy of a marriage or marriage duration document (if any)
  • A copy of child/children (if any)
  • Certificate of work activity (copy of the employment record)

The following list should be paid to the last position, as a copy of the employment record should be certified at the previous place of work. You can do this at the same time as obtaining the characteristics.

Action and Photo Card

  • Excerpt on criminal record (excerpt from the informative-analytical system "Accounting for information about bringing a person to criminal liability and availability of criminal record")

Completes a list of documents set of photo cards:

  • The standard block of eight photo cards 3 × 4 (six of them will be attached to the Personal Code in the CPC of the JV, others will be needed to pass the Military Medical Commission)
