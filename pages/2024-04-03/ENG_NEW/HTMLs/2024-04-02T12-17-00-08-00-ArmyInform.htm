The Ministry of Defense of Ukraine, together with the state rear operator (DOT) and the heads of food services, participated in the online meeting of the National Agency for Prevention of Corruption (NAPC).

About it reportsfieldDefense Office.

The purpose is to develop effective anti -corruption fuses in the purchase of food for the Armed Forces of Ukraine, as well as to improve the interaction between the participants of this process.

“Purchase transparency is the key to financial efficiency of budgetary and trust in the Ministry of Defense. That is why we are building a new procurement architecture that meets NATO practices. We are obliged to provide the Armed Forces in a timely manner, ”said Deputy Minister of Defense of Ukraine Dmitry Klimenkov.

During the meeting, the representatives of the state operator emphasized the importance of building a system of rearranging on the basis of integrity and steps in this direction.

“Our task is not only to meet the needs of the military, but also to meet the request of society for transparency, integrity, elimination of corruption risks. Construction of an effective food supply system requires the inclusion of headquarters of military units, their responsible and clear work, ”said Dmitry Bigunets, director of risk management and compliance.

NAPC Deputy Chairman Yaroslav Lyubchenko noted that now Ukrainian experts, together with NATO representatives, are developing recommendations for improving the defense procurement system within the framework of a strategic inspection of a system of defense procurement of a comprehensive NATO assistance package for Ukraine.

“At the same time, the NAPC begins an analysis of corruption risks in this area - it will be involved in all stakeholders and international experts. As a result, we will provide recommendations that will contribute to the development of integrity in defense purchases and the implementation of the reform of the Ministry of Defense have already started, ”he added.

According to the results of the meeting, the DOT team will process proposals to build effective interaction to ensure the continuity of supply.

As reported by the Armyinform, the first stage of food supplies from marked DOT supplierssuccessfully completed. More than 300 trucks have already been sent to military units throughout Ukraine.
