The Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine approved an agreement with the Romanian government to open a new international crossing point across the border between the settlements of Bila Tserkva (Transcarpathian region, Ukraine) and Sigett Marmotii (Romania).

About thisreportedPrime Minister Denis Shmigal at a government meeting.

"Ukraine and Romania are building an extremely profitable partnership, and we aim to constantly deepen it," he said.

In general, according to the head of the government, in the western direction it is planned to create 9 new border crossing points and to upgrade another 13.

"This will give more opportunities for Ukrainian exports and accelerate our sectoral integration into the European Union," Denis Shmigal summarized.

As reportedArmyinform, in the Belgorod-Dnestrovsky detachment told about changes in the border regime.
