The International Criminal Court issued warrants for the arrest of two Russian high -ranking commanders - Sergei Kobylyash and Viktor Sokolov for crimes committed at least on October 10, 2022 until at least 9 March 2023.

This is stated in a message onSiteMKS.

Sergey Kobylash-Lieutenant General of the Armed Forces of Russia, Commander of the Far Aviation of Air Force.

Viktor Sokolov-Admiral of the Navy of Russia, Commander of the Black Sea Fleet of the Russian Federation.

Both are charged with a martial crime that was involved in attacks on civilian objects (Article 8 (2) (b) (II) of the Rome Statute), a war crime that was in causing excessive accidental harm to civilians or harm to civilians (Article 8 (2) (B) (IV) of the Roman Statute).

They are also charged with a crime against humanity, which consisted in the inhuman acts provided for in Article 7 (1) (K) of the Roman Statute.

"There are reasonable grounds to believe that they bear individual criminal liability for the above crimes for committing them jointly and/or through other persons and gave orders to commit crimes and/or did not exercise proper control over the forces that were in charge of them." , - the statement said.

Both arrest warrants were issued on the basis of the petitions filed by the prosecution.

The Chamber of Pre -trial Investigation II believes that there are sufficient reasons to believe that both suspects are responsible for missile strokes caused by the forces under their command at the facilities the year.

During this period, a campaign of strokes on numerous power plants and substations, which were carried out by the Russian Armed Forces in different places of Ukraine, took place.

The II ward II found that there were sufficient reasons to believe that probable blows were directed against civilian objects, and for those objects that could at the appropriate time qualify as military objects, expected accidental harm and damage caused to civilian The population would be clearly excessive compared to the expected military advantage.

Thus, there are sufficient reasons to believe that the suspects are also responsible for the crime against humanity "other inhuman acts ... Deliberate causes severe suffering or serious bodily harm, or mental or physical health."

The content of warrants is issued under the stamp "secretly" in order to protect witnesses and to ensure investigation.

But the House II believes that in the interests of justice, it is possible to publicly disclose information about the availability of orders, names of suspects, crimes for which orders were issued, and measures of responsibility.

We will remind, in March last year the Chamber of Pre -trial proceedings of the II International Criminal Court issued a warrant for the arrest of President of Russia Vladimir Putin in connection with the export of Ukrainian children from the occupied territories of Ukraine.
